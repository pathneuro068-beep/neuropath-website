About us

Behaviour support that feels human.

NeuroPath Behavioural Services Pty Ltd is a positive behaviour support practice working with children, young people, and adults with disability. We believe behaviour is communication — and that lasting change comes from understanding the person, their environment, and the people who love them.

Our practitioners are trained in the principles of Positive Behaviour Support (PBS), trauma-informed practice, and the NDIS Quality and Safeguards framework. We work collaboratively with families, schools, support workers, and allied health teams to design supports that are respectful, restraint-free, and built to last.

Person-centred

Plans built around the person, their goals, and the people in their world.

Evidence-based

Functional assessment, data, and PBS principles guide every recommendation.

Capacity-building

We aim to make ourselves less needed over time, not more.

Services

How we can help

Every plan is tailored — but most engagements start with one of the services below. We're happy to talk through what's likely to be the best fit for the participant.

Functional Behaviour Assessment

A structured assessment to understand the function of behaviours of concern — what they communicate, what triggers them, and what helps. Includes observation, interviews, data review, and a written report.

Positive Behaviour Support Plans

Interim and comprehensive PBSPs aligned with NDIS Commission requirements. Plans are written in plain language and co-designed with the participant, family, and support team.

NDIS Behaviour Support

Registered behaviour support services for NDIS participants, including implementation support, reportable incident review, and reduction of restrictive practices over time.

Environmental & Routine Modification

Practical changes to the spaces, routines, and sensory environments that shape day-to-day life. Small adjustments that reduce stress and make success more likely.

Training & Capacity Building

Tailored training for support workers, families, and schools — from proactive strategies and de-escalation through to implementing a specific participant's behaviour support plan.
